Workflow
This diagram illustrates a typical vision-based 3D measurement and modelling workflow.
It starts with the acquisition of data, which can be either images only (as is commonly the case with SfM in computer vision) or images with associated geo-referencing data from positioning systems such as GNSS and inertial systems. This combination is typical of aerial photogrammetry applications. Other input data may include classical odometry in the case of SLAM approaches.
Image processing can be performed in real-time (online SLAM in robotics) or in post-processing (photogrammetry, SfM in computer vision and full or off-line SLAM).
Usually in a SLAM process the final result is a (sparse or dense) 'map' of the environment and the poses of the agent (robot equipped with a camera and possibly other sensors). A SfM process can be followed by the MVS (multi-view stereo) step, the result of which is a dense model of the scene. These steps are also performed in a photogrammetric process, which can often include the generation of an orthophotomosaic.
